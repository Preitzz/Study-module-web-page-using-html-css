PROCESS SYNCHRONIZATION

The process synchronisation means to coordinate simultaneous threads or processes inorder to avoid accidentally happening of race conditions and to get the correct runtime order. To avoid the race conditions ,the process synchronisation resulted critical section problem.


.. .. .. .. .. Race Condition.. .. .. .. ..

Several processes access and manipulate the same data simultaneosly/ concurrently and the outcome of the execution depends on the particular order in which the access takes place, is called a race condition. Inorder to protect against race condition, we need to ensure that only one process at a time can be manipulating the variable counter . To make such a guarantee the processes needs to be synchronized in some way . A race condition is an undesirable situation that occurs when a device or system attempts to perform two or more operations at the same time, but because of the nature of the device or system, the operations must be done in the proper sequence to be done correctly.

    Race condition occurs due to :
  • timing and which process starts
  • Interleaving,one cant predict what will happen next while the processes are executing.
    Solutions to race conditions:
  1. disable interrupts
  2. lock variables
  3. Strict alternation

CRITICAL SECTION

  • The critical section problem arises because of coordination of the interaction of processes. Every process has critical section
  • For data access synchronisation,the critical section is used.Cant block a process.So can only be used for mutex.
  • It is a control structure.Over a shared variable ,it is used to implement convenient mutual exclusion(only 1 process is executed in its critical region. ) .

Each process has critical section segment of code Process may be changing common variables, updating table,writing file, etc. When one process in critical section, no other may be in its critical section. Ie, no two processes are allowed to execute in the critical section at the same time. Critical section problem is to design protocol to solve this. In a related situation, a critical section may be used to ensure that a shared resource, for example, a printer, can only be accessed by one process at a time.


Solution to critical section must satisfy 3 conditions:
1. Mutual Exclusion :If process Pi is executing in its critical section, then no other processes can be executing in their critical sections
2. Progress : If no process is executing in its critical section and some processes wish to enter their critical sections, then only those processes that are not executing in their remainder sections can participate in deciding which will enter its critical section next, and this selection cannot be postponed indefinitely.
3.Bounded waiting :A bound must exist on the number of times that other processes are allowed to enter their critical sections after a process has made a request to enter its critical section and before that request is granted.
